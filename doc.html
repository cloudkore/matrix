Welcome to the Matrix™ Dashboard, your personal, secure space built with privacy and convenience in mind. Inspired by the tactical world of Ninja School Online, our dashboard provides essential tools to manage your digital life with robust security measures.

What is the Matrix™ Dashboard?

The Matrix™ Dashboard is the central hub of your Matrix™ web application. It's designed to give you easy access to critical personal tools like:

  • Secure Notes: Store important thoughts, reminders, or game strategies.
  • Password Manager: Safely keep track of your login credentials for various services.
  • Ephemeral Files Storage: Upload and manage temporary files securely.
  • Profile Management: Customize your in-app identity.

Your Data, Your Security: Encryption Explained

At the core of the Matrix™ Dashboard's security is end-to-end encryption. This means your sensitive data (notes, passwords, and files) is scrambled and made unreadable *before* it ever leaves your device and is stored on our servers.

Here's how we ensure your privacy:

  • Master Password: When you first set up your account or access sensitive features, you'll be prompted to create or enter a "Master Password." This password is never stored on our servers.
  • Key Derivation: Your Master Password is used to derive a unique encryption key on your device using a strong, industry-standard algorithm called PBKDF2 (Password-Based Key Derivation Function 2) with 200,000 iterations and SHA256 hashing. This process ensures that even if our databases were compromised, your Master Password cannot be reverse-engineered, and your data remains unreadable.
  • Session-Based Key: For your convenience and continued security, this derived encryption key is stored temporarily in your browser's sessionStorage. This allows you to access your encrypted data throughout your current session without re-entering your Master Password for every action. However, once you log out or close your browser, this session key is automatically cleared.
  • No Server Access: Because your data is encrypted on your device using a key *only you know*, no one else, not even the Matrix™ administrators or developers, can access or read your sensitive notes, passwords, or files. This provides a strong guarantee of privacy and confidentiality.

Important: Due to this robust encryption method, if you forget your Master Password, there is no way to reset it or recover your encrypted data. We recommend storing your Master Password in a safe, offline location.

Dashboard Features

1. Profile Management

Upon logging in, you'll enter the Dashboard setup section if it's your first time or if your profile isn't complete.

  • Username: Set your unique username that will be displayed on your dashboard.
  • Avatar Selection: Choose from a selection of avatars to personalize your profile. You can easily browse through available avatars using the "Previous" and "Next" buttons.
  • Save Profile: Once you've set your username and chosen an avatar, click "Save Profile" to finalize your settings and proceed to the main dashboard.

2. Secure Notes

The Secure Notes feature allows you to jot down any text-based information you want to keep private.

  • Accessing Notes: Click the "Notes" button on your dashboard. If your encryption key isn't active, you will be prompted to enter your Master Password to unlock this feature.
  • Adding a Note: Type your note into the provided input field and click "Save Note." Your note will be encrypted before being stored securely.
  • Viewing Notes: All your saved notes are displayed in a list, with the most recent notes appearing first. Each note includes a timestamp indicating when it was saved.
  • Deleting a Note: To remove a note, click the "Delete" button next to it. You will be asked to confirm your action to prevent accidental deletions.

3. Password Manager

Securely store your login credentials for various online services.

  • Accessing Password Manager: Click the "Password Manager" button on your dashboard. Similar to notes, you'll need to enter your Master Password if the encryption key is not active.
  • Adding an Entry: Fill in the "Service Name" (e.g., "Facebook"), "Username," and "Password" fields. The password will be encrypted before storage. Click "Save Entry" to add it to your manager.
  • Viewing Entries: Your saved password entries are listed, showing the service name, username, and the decrypted password.
  • Deleting an Entry: Use the "Delete" button next to an entry to remove it. A confirmation prompt will appear before permanent deletion.

4. Ephemeral Files Storage

Upload and manage files that you need to store temporarily and securely.

  • Accessing Files: Click the "Ephemeral Files" button. You will be prompted for your Master Password if your encryption key is not available.
  • Uploading Files:
    • Click "Choose File" and select the file from your device.
    • File Size Limit: Please note there is a file size limit of 5 MB per file. If your file exceeds this, the upload will be prevented.
    • Click "Upload File" to initiate the upload.
    • A progress bar will show the upload status.
  • Viewing and Downloading Files: Your uploaded files are listed, showing the file name and upload timestamp. Click the "Download" button to retrieve a file to your device.
  • Deleting Files: Click the "Delete" button next to a file. A confirmation dialog will appear before the file is permanently removed.

5. Language Selection

The Matrix™ Dashboard supports multiple languages for a personalized user experience.

  • Change Language: Use the dropdown selector to choose your preferred language. The dashboard elements will instantly update to the selected language. Your language preference is saved in your browser's local storage for future visits.

6. Account Management

  • Logout: Click the "Logout" button to securely sign out of your Matrix™ account. This will clear your encryption key from the session and redirect you to the login page.
  • Delete Account:
    • If you wish to permanently remove your Matrix™ account and all associated data, click the "Delete Account" button.
    • Confirmation is required: You will be asked to confirm this irreversible action.
    • Comprehensive Deletion: This process ensures the complete removal of:
      • All your uploaded files and their metadata from our cloud storage.
      • All your notes and password manager entries from our database.
      • Your user profile document.
      • Your Firebase authentication account.
    • Recent Login Requirement: For security, Firebase may require you to have recently logged in to delete your account. If this is the case, you'll be informed and may be logged out to re-authenticate before attempting deletion again.

Underlying Technologies

The Matrix™ application leverages powerful and secure cloud technologies to provide its services:

  • Google Firebase: Used for user authentication (managing your login and user sessions) and for storing your encrypted notes and password manager entries in its Firestore database. Your user profile data (username, avatar, encryption salt, and master password hash) is also stored here.
  • Supabase: Utilized for secure and ephemeral file storage. File uploads, listings, and deletions are handled via Supabase Edge Functions for efficient and secure operations.
  • Cryptographic Libraries (CryptoJS): Powers the robust encryption and decryption of your sensitive data using AES encryption and PBKDF2 for key derivation.

Accessibility and Cross-Platform Compatibility

Matrix™ is designed as a web application, making it accessible across a variety of platforms:

  • Desktop: You can access the Matrix™ Dashboard seamlessly on Windows and Linux desktop operating systems using any modern web browser.
  • Mobile: The web application is designed to be responsive, providing a good user experience on mobile devices, including Android smartphones and tablets.
  • Legacy Devices (J2ME Emulators): While not officially supported for optimal performance or full feature compatibility, the web-based nature of Matrix™ means it *may* be accessible to varying degrees on J2ME (Java Platform, Micro Edition) emulators, although specific functionality and rendering cannot be guaranteed due to the limitations of these older environments. For the best experience, we recommend modern browsers on up-to-date operating systems.
